The Harrington Collection

Greenville, DE 9am – 4pm · Sat & Sun In-Person

A distinguished two-day sale from the estate of the late Eleanor Harrington, longtime Greenville resident and dedicated collector. Highlights include a full service of Georgian sterling silver, an extensive suite of botanical engravings, and an extraordinary Chippendale walnut secretary desk dated approximately 1780. Mid-Century glass, Orientalia, and a large library of first editions round out this exceptional offering.

Parking & arrival

Complimentary parking on the circular drive; additional street parking on a first-come basis. Please enter through the main foyer—signage will guide you to the dedicated sale rooms on the first and second floors.

Payment & packing

We accept cash, major credit cards, and approved checks with valid identification. Porter service is available for large furniture; bring blankets or moving supplies if you plan to take sizable pieces.

House rules

No large bags in the main salons; children are welcome under supervision. Numbers may be issued at opening on busy mornings—arrive early for the best selection of silver and smalls.

Bellhaven Manor Dispersal

Kennett Square, PA 10am – 3pm · Saturday only In-Person

The contents of a meticulously kept 1920s manor house, furnished entirely in the Art Deco style its original owners loved. Expect custom-built ebonised case pieces, at least four signed Tiffany-style lamps, an extensive collection of vintage costume jewellery including signed pieces by Miriam Haskell and Coro, and an elegant 2,000-volume personal library spanning travel, natural history, and the decorative arts.

Parking & arrival

Park in the marked visitor bays along the west lawn. Follow the gravel path to the side entrance—this sale concentrates on the ground floor and lower gallery for easier flow.

Payment & packing

Cash, card, and bank check welcome. A volunteer desk can hold purchases while you continue browsing; jewellery cases are staffed at all times.

House rules

Single-day sale with continuous admission; no preview day. Flash photography in the lamp gallery is politely declined to protect iridescent finishes.

The Merriweather Estate

Media, PA 9am – 5pm · Sat & Sun In-Person

Three generations of passionate collectors come together in this major two-day event. The Merriweather family amassed a formidable collection spanning Asian export porcelain from the 17th–19th centuries, a gallery of ancestor and landscape oil portraits, full Victorian parlour furniture suites in horsehair and mahogany, and a walled garden's worth of cast-iron and limestone statuary. Not to be missed.

Parking & arrival

Shuttle carts run from overflow parking at the parish lot three blocks south on Saturday peak hours. Garden statuary may be viewed outdoors weather permitting—wear sensible footwear.

Payment & packing

We accept cash, card, and wire for high-value porcelain lots. Crating partners are on call Sunday afternoon for larger garden pieces.

House rules

Two full days; Sunday often sees the best availability on case furniture. Food and drink remain outside on the stone terrace.

West Chester Collector's Clearance

West Chester, PA 9am – 3pm · Saturday only In-Person

A focused single-day sale from the estate of a retired university professor with decades of focused collecting in maps, antiquarian books, scientific instruments, and vintage photography. Highlights include a folio of hand-coloured 18th-century maps, a collection of 19th-century stereographs, and an outstanding group of early astronomical instruments in original fitted cases.

Parking & arrival

Municipal garage two blocks east offers validated parking with any purchase over $100—ask at the registration desk. The sale occupies the town house library and upper study.

Payment & packing

Cash and card preferred for paper ephemera; personal checks accepted from established clients. Gloves are provided for handling loose atlas plates.

House rules

Compact, scholarly sale—please keep voices low in instrument rooms. No backpacks; lockers available in the hall.

The Ashford Park Auction Preview

Wilmington, DE 10am – 4pm · Preview Only Preview Event

A special preview event for our summer auction partnership with Ashford Park Auction House. View items in person before online bidding opens on June 14th. Catalogue available at the door. Highlights include a documented Quaker furniture suite from Chester County, important Delaware silver, and a group of early Pennsylvania watercolours.

Parking & arrival

Valet and self-park lanes at the auction house main entrance. This is a preview day only—no sales transactions on site; staff can register you for absentee and online bidding.

Catalogue & registration

Printed catalogues while supplies last; digital copies scan at the lobby kiosks. Condition reports may be requested at the specialist desk for Delaware silver and furniture lots.

House rules

Touching of framed works is limited to staff assistance. Online bidding for this sale opens June 14, 2026—see Ashford Park's site for real-time schedules.
